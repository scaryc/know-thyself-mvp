# Paramedic Master - Scenario Creation Demonstration

## About This Demo

This document demonstrates how medical educators interact with Paramedic Master to create high-quality emergency medical training scenarios. The conversation below shows a typical scenario development process from initial concept to complete implementation.

**Platform:** Know Thyself - AI-Powered Medical Education  
**Agent:** Paramedic Master - Specialized Medical Scenario Architect  
**Use Case:** Creating realistic patient simulations for paramedic training

---

## Sample Conversation: Creating an Asthma Scenario

### Exchange 1: Initial Request

**Medical Educator:**
> I need an asthma exacerbation scenario for intermediate paramedic students. This will be used in our respiratory emergencies module. Students should be challenged to manage a deteriorating patient who isn't responding well to initial treatment.

---

### Exchange 2: Clarifying Questions

**Paramedic Master:**
> I'll help you create an effective asthma scenario that challenges clinical reasoning. Let me gather some details to ensure this meets your educational goals:
>
> **Patient Demographics & Context:**
> - What age range and patient profile would work best? (Adult, pediatric, elderly with comorbidities?)
> - Any specific social or environmental factors? (Home setting, workplace, specific triggers?)
> - Geographic context for transport decisions?
>
> **Severity & Progression:**
> - Initial presentation severity: Moderate distress? Severe with impending respiratory failure?
> - How quickly should the patient deteriorate if untreated? (3, 5, or 10 minutes?)
> - Should they respond poorly to first-line treatment to challenge decision-making?
>
> **Learning Objectives:**
> - Primary focus: Beta-agonist administration? Recognition of severe asthma? Escalation decisions?
> - Should students face complications like hypotension from beta-agonists?
> - Any specific assessment skills you want to emphasize? (Lung sounds, work of breathing, mental status changes?)
>
> **Protocol Considerations:**
> - Which formulary are you using? (I can work with Slovak EMS protocols and the 2018 Slovak formulary)
> - Any specific medications or procedures students should consider beyond standard salbutamol/ipratropium?

---

### Exchange 3: Educator Provides Details

**Medical Educator:**
> Great questions. Here's what I'm thinking:
>
> - **Patient:** Young adult, late 20s, at their apartment. Known asthmatic but hasn't been compliant with controller medications. Triggered by a friend's cat.
> - **Severity:** Start moderate-severe but deteriorate to critical within 5 minutes if not treated properly. They should respond somewhat to salbutamol but not enough - need multiple doses and possibly IV magnesium consideration.
> - **Learning Focus:** Recognition of severe asthma signs, proper beta-agonist dosing, knowing when to escalate treatment, managing anxiety component
> - **Protocols:** Yes, use Slovak EMS protocols and the 2018 formulary
> - **Challenge Element:** Patient is very anxious which worsens their breathing. Students need to manage both the medical and psychological aspects.

---

### Exchange 4: Initial Scenario Outline

**Paramedic Master:**
> Perfect. Here's the scenario structure I'm designing:
>
> **SCENARIO: "Feline Trigger" - Acute Asthma Exacerbation**
>
> **Patient Profile:**
> - Martina Kováčová, 28-year-old female
> - Location: 3rd floor apartment, Bratislava-Petržalka
> - Known asthmatic, poor medication adherence
> - Triggered 30 minutes ago by friend's cat
> - Took her rescue inhaler (2 puffs) with minimal relief
>
> **Initial Presentation (State 1):**
> - Sitting forward, tripod position, visibly distressed
> - Speaking in 3-4 word sentences
> - SpO2: 91%, HR: 118, RR: 28, BP: 145/92
> - Diffuse expiratory wheezing, prolonged expiration
> - Using accessory muscles
> - Anxious, fearful expression
>
> **Progression Design:**
> - **5 minutes untreated:** Deteriorates to State 2 (SpO2 → 88%, increased work of breathing)
> - **10 minutes untreated:** Progresses to State 3 (SpO2 → 85%, exhaustion beginning)
> - **15 minutes untreated:** Critical State 4 (silent chest, altered mental status, respiratory failure)
>
> **Treatment Responses:**
> - Salbutamol nebulization: Improves symptoms moderately but not fully (typical partial responder)
> - Oxygen therapy: SpO2 improvement but respiratory distress persists
> - Ipratropium bromide: Additive benefit when combined with salbutamol
> - Corticosteroids (hydrocortisone IV/methylprednisolone): Delayed benefit (prevents deterioration)
> - Magnesium sulfate IV: Significant bronchodilation for severe cases
>
> **Key Learning Elements:**
> - Recognition of severe asthma indicators (tripod position, inability to speak, accessory muscle use)
> - Understanding partial vs. complete response to treatment
> - Anxiety management while maintaining clinical control
> - Knowing when to call for ALS backup or consider magnesium
> - Proper reassessment timing
>
> **Performance Evaluation:**
> - Critical actions: Oxygen administration, bronchodilator therapy, position of comfort
> - Time-sensitive tasks: First bronchodilator within 3 minutes
> - Challenge questions: Built-in decision points about escalation and anxiety management
>
> Does this align with your vision? Any aspects you'd like me to adjust?

---

### Exchange 5: Educator Requests Modifications

**Medical Educator:**
> This looks excellent! A few refinements:
>
> 1. Can you make the deterioration faster - maybe 3 minutes instead of 5? I want more urgency.
> 2. Add a complication: After the second salbutamol dose, she develops mild tremors and tachycardia (140s). Students should recognize this as a beta-agonist side effect but not stop treatment.
> 3. Include a decision point about calling for ALS backup - when should they recognize this is beyond BLS capabilities?
> 4. Can you add some incorrect medication options students might wrongly consider? Like morphine for anxiety or promethazine?

---

### Exchange 6: Incorporating Changes

**Paramedic Master:**
> Excellent modifications. I'm updating the scenario:
>
> **Timeline Acceleration:**
> - State transitions now at 3/6/10 minutes for increased urgency
> - This creates appropriate pressure without being unrealistic
>
> **Beta-Agonist Complication:**
> - After 2nd salbutamol dose (typically around 10 minutes):
>   - HR increases to 142 bpm
>   - Fine tremors in hands
>   - Patient reports "feeling shaky"
> - This is a NORMAL side effect - challenge question asks if they should continue bronchodilators
> - Correct answer: Continue treatment; benefits outweigh side effects in severe asthma
>
> **ALS Escalation Decision Point:**
> - Built into State 3 (severe) criteria:
>   - If SpO2 < 88% despite high-flow oxygen
>   - If patient requires 3rd bronchodilator dose with poor response
>   - If signs of exhaustion/altered mental status appear
> - Performance tracker awards points for early ALS request
>
> **Inappropriate Medication Library:**
> Adding comprehensive "wrong medication" responses with educational harm levels:
>
> - **Morphine** (CRITICAL HARM): Respiratory depression in compromised patient - causes immediate deterioration
> - **Promethazine** (HIGH HARM): Anticholinergic effects, dries secretions, sedation worsens respiratory status
> - **Diazepam** (HIGH HARM): Respiratory depressant, removes compensatory anxiety-driven respiratory effort
> - **Diphenhydramine** (MODERATE HARM): Similar to promethazine but less severe
> - **Furosemide** (LOW HARM): Not indicated but doesn't directly harm
>
> Each includes specific physiological response explaining WHY it's wrong - building clinical reasoning.
>
> I'm now finalizing the complete scenario with these modifications.

---

### Exchange 7: Final Presentation

**Paramedic Master:**
> The scenario is complete. I've generated a comprehensive blueprint including:
>
> ✅ **Complete patient profile** with medical history, social context, and environmental setup  
> ✅ **Four progressive patient states** with detailed vital signs and clinical presentations  
> ✅ **Dynamic response system** for all appropriate medications (salbutamol, ipratropium, O2, corticosteroids, magnesium)  
> ✅ **Complication modeling** for beta-agonist side effects at appropriate timing  
> ✅ **15+ inappropriate medications** with harm-level categorization and educational responses  
> ✅ **Hidden findings structure** for systematic physical examination  
> ✅ **Performance evaluation criteria** with time-based scoring and critical actions  
> ✅ **Universal challenge questions** covering complications, communication, ethics, and time pressure  
> ✅ **ALS escalation decision points** with clear clinical triggers
>
> The scenario is ready for integration with the General Prompt Agent. Students will experience a realistic, high-pressure asthma case that challenges both technical skills and clinical judgment.
>
> **Total scenario length:** ~2,100 lines of structured JSON  
> **Educational depth:** Supports 30-45 minute simulation session  
> **Difficulty level:** Intermediate to Advanced
>
> The complete file is: `ASTHMA_EXACERBATION_KOVACOVA_v1.json`
