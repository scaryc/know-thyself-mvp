## Problem We Solve

### The Medical Simulation Content Bottleneck

Traditional medical simulation scenario development faces fundamental scalability barriers:

**Technical Complexity Barrier**
- Scenarios require complex JSON data structures (500-2000+ lines per scenario)
- Authors need medical expertise AND technical programming knowledge
- Data architecture decisions (state machines, medication responses, scoring algorithms) require both domains
- Most medical educators lack JSON authoring skills; most developers lack emergency medicine expertise

**Resource Intensity**
- Traditional timeline: **6-12 weeks per high-quality scenario**
- Required team: Medical SME ($150-200/hr) + Developer ($100-150/hr) + Instructional Designer ($75-100/hr)
- Cost per scenario: **$15,000-30,000** depending on complexity
- Bottleneck: SMEs can't create content themselves; developers can't verify medical accuracy

**Quality Inconsistency**
- Manual authoring creates structural variations between scenarios
- No systematic validation of medical accuracy
- Protocol updates require finding original authors and developers
- Knowledge loss when team members leave

**Scalability Crisis**
- Building 50-scenario library traditionally: **50-100 person-weeks** of SME time
- Each scenario is a custom development project
- Content creation velocity limited by development team capacity
- Cannot respond quickly to new protocols or emerging medical conditions

**What Happens Without Solution**
- Platforms launch with 5-10 scenarios and struggle to expand
- Competitors with larger content libraries dominate market
- Institutions build scenarios in-house at enormous cost
- Educational quality suffers from limited scenario diversity
- Student training remains narrow due to content scarcity
